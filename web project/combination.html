Combination Formula Description

Combinations, often referred to as "n choose r," are a fundamental concept in combinatorics and probability theory. They represent the number of ways to choose r items from a set of n items without regard to the order of selection. Combinations are used in various applications, such as forming teams, selecting items, and more.

Combination Formula

The combination formula calculates the number of combinations (C) for selecting r items from a set of n items:

C(n, r) = n! / (r! * (n - r)!)

Where:

  • C(n, r) represents the number of combinations of n items taken r at a time.
  • n! denotes the factorial of n, which is the product of all positive integers from 1 to n.
  • r! denotes the factorial of r.
  • (n - r)! denotes the factorial of the difference between n and r.
