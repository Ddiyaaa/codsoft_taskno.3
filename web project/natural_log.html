Natural Log Calculator

The natural logarithm, denoted as ln(x), is a mathematical function that calculates the logarithm to the base e (Euler's number) of a given number x. The formula for the natural logarithm is:

ln(x)

Where:

  • ln(x): This represents the natural logarithm of the number x.
  • e (Euler's number): Euler's number is a mathematical constant approximately equal to 2.71828.
  • x: This is the number for which you want to find the natural logarithm.

The natural logarithm is widely used in mathematics, science, and engineering for various calculations, including exponential growth, decay, and rates of change.
