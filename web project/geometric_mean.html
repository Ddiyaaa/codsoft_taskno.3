Geometric Mean Formula Description

The geometric mean is a mathematical measure of central tendency that is often used to find the average of a set of positive numbers. It is particularly useful when dealing with values that grow exponentially, such as investment returns or population growth rates.

Mean Formula

The geometric mean (GM) of a set of positive numbers x₁, x₂, x₃, ..., xₙ can be calculated using the geometric mean formula:

GM = √(x₁ * x₂ * x₃ * ... * xₙ)

Where:

  • GM represents the geometric mean of the numbers.
  • x₁, x₂, x₃, ..., xₙ are the positive numbers in the dataset.
