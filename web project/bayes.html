Bayes' Theorem Calculator

Bayes' theorem allows us to update our prior probability (P(A)) based on new evidence (P(B)) and the likelihood of the evidence given the event (P(B|A)). The formula is:

P(A|B) = P(A) â‹… P(B|A) / P(B)

Where:

  • Prior Probability (P(A)): This is the probability of an event A occurring before taking any new evidence into account.
  • Likelihood (P(B|A)): This is the probability of observing evidence B given that event A has occurred.
  • Evidence (P(B)): This is the total probability of observing evidence B.
  • P(A|B): This is the posterior probability, the probability of event A occurring after considering the evidence B.
