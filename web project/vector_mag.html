Vector Magnitude Calculator

In physics and mathematics, the magnitude of a vector represents its length or size. It's calculated using the formula:

|V| = √(Vx2 + Vy2 + Vz2)

Where:

  • |V| (Magnitude of Vector V): This is the length or size of the vector V.
  • Vx, Vy, Vz: These are the components of vector V along the X, Y, and Z axes, respectively.

The magnitude of a vector is a fundamental concept in vector analysis and is used to determine the length of vectors in various applications, including physics, engineering, and computer graphics.
