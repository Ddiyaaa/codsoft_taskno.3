Unit Vector Calculator

In mathematics and physics, a unit vector is a vector with a magnitude (length) of 1. It's used to represent the direction of a vector without regard to its scale. The formula for finding the unit vector is:

The unit vector is (i, j, k)

Where:

  • The unit vector: Represents the direction of the original vector with a magnitude of 1.
  • i, j, k: Are the components of the unit vector along the X, Y, and Z axes, respectively.

Unit vectors are used in various applications, including physics, computer graphics, and engineering, to describe direction without altering the vector's length.
